# Lesson 2 was too dense; learner needs ONE idea per lesson, analogy-first

Lesson 2 packed three big concepts (consistency, failover, the nines math) into a single page. The learner found it hard and said "too much at once." They told me directly that what helps most is **a simpler everyday analogy first, then map it back to systems.**

**Implications for ALL future lessons:**
- **One concept per lesson.** Hard cap. If a Primer section has multiple sub-ideas, split it across multiple short lessons. Coverage speed is NOT the goal — retention is (see [[0002-retention-is-the-real-goal]]).
- **Lead with a non-tech analogy**, then map each piece of the analogy to the technical idea, THEN the Django bridge. Analogy → mechanism → real example.
- Lesson 2 is being rebuilt to cover ONLY consistency. Failover and the nines math will become their own separate small lessons.
- This supersedes my earlier instinct that Lesson-2-depth was "about right" — it was too much. See [[NOTES.md]].
