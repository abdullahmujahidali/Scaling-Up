# Retention (not coverage) is the real goal; follow one source in order

The learner stated two steering decisions directly. (1) **Path:** follow ONE source section-by-section rather than me choosing a top-down curriculum — they explicitly don't want topics decided for them. Default spine is the System Design Primer (already in progress). (2) **Goal:** they forget easily and want lessons that "stick after one read," and they're worried AI is eroding their own recall — especially dangerous for system design, which must live in their head for interviews/design reviews, not be outsourced to AI.

**Implications for teaching:**
- Build spaced retrieval INTO the course so the learner never has to organize review themselves: every new lesson opens with a ~30-second recall of prior lesson(s). This is the structural answer to "make it stick."
- Weight lessons toward active recall + reasoning-out-loud over information dumps.
- Don't free-style a syllabus — let them point at the next section, or march the chosen source in order. See [[NOTES.md]] and [[MISSION.md]].
